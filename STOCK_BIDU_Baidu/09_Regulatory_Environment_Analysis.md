## Key Regulatory Factors

### 1. AI Regulations (Primary Risk Factor)

#### 1.1 Generative AI Service Requirements

**[FACT] Interim Measures for Generative AI Services**
- **Effective Date**: August 15, 2023
- **Regulator**: Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC)
- **Scope**: All generative AI services offered to the public in China
- **Status**: As of December 2024, **302 generative AI services** have been filed and approved, with **105 services** fully registered

**Key Requirements**:
1. **Content Compliance**:
   - Must uphold socialist values
   - Must respect morality and ethics
   - Cannot threaten state security or social order
   - Cannot produce prohibited content (political, violent, etc.)

2. **Security & Technical Requirements**:
   - Secure training processes
   - Follow annotation security specifications
   - Implement both explicit and implicit labeling for AI-generated content
   - Mandatory user consent requirements
   - Mandatory security reviews before public launch

3. **New Labeling Rules** (Effective September 1, 2025):
   - Explicit labeling obligations on AI-generated content
   - Implicit labeling obligations
   - Applies to "internet information" services

**Impact on Baidu**:
- Baidu's ERNIE Bot (文心一言) required CAC approval before public launch
- Ongoing compliance costs for content filtering and moderation
- Potential limitations on AI capabilities due to content restrictions
- Mandatory investment in content moderation infrastructure

**Confidence**: High (based on regulatory documents)

- Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC) official announcements

### 2. Data Privacy & Content Regulations

#### 2.1 Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL)

**[FACT] PIPL Implementation**
- **Effective Date**: November 1, 2021
- **Scope**: All processing of personal information in China
- **Key Requirements**:
  - Explicit user consent for data collection
  - Data minimization principle
  - Purpose limitation
  - User rights to access and delete data

**Impact on Baidu**:
- Search engine collects extensive user data (queries, location, behavior)
- Must implement robust consent management systems
- Cross-border data transfer restrictions affect global R&D collaboration
