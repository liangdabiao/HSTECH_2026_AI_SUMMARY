- **Computing Power Subscriptions**: +143% year-over-year in Q4

#### Strategic Pivot (2025)
1. **Free Access Model** (April 1, 2025)
   - Ernie Bot became free for all users
   - Strategic goal: Boost adoption and competitiveness

2. **Open Source Initiative** (June 30, 2025)
   - Next-generation Ernie model to be open-sourced
   - Positions Baidu as competitor to OpenAI and DeepSeek

**Analysis**:
- [FACT] Baidu targeting significant AI Cloud revenue growth (30B Yuan by 2025)
- [FACT] Strong growth in computing power subscriptions (+143% YoY)
- [ANALYSIS] Strategic shift: Prioritizing ecosystem expansion over direct monetization
- [ANALYSIS] Free + open-source strategy suggests competitive pressure from emerging models (DeepSeek, etc.)
- [OPINION] This pivot may pressure 2025 revenue growth but could strengthen long-term position

## 4. Recent Strategic Changes (Last 12-18 Months)

### 4.1 Timeline of Key Changes

| Date | Change | Strategic Implication |
|------|--------|----------------------|
| Aug 2023 | Ernie Bot public launch | Initial consumer AI entry |
| Dec 2023-Apr 2024 | MAU doubles (100M → 200M) | Strong user adoption |
| Nov 2024 | 430M total users reached | Mass adoption achieved |
| Apr 1, 2025 | Ernie Bot becomes free | Shift to ecosystem model |
| Jun 30, 2025 | Ernie model open-sourced | Competitive positioning |

### 4.2 Strategic Interpretation

**What This Signals**:
1. **Competitive Pressure**: Free/open-source pivot suggests pressure from:
   - DeepSeek (emerging Chinese AI model)
   - OpenAI (global standard)
   - Other Chinese tech giants (Alibaba, Tencent)

2. **Ecosystem Priority**: Baidu choosing market share over immediate revenue
   - Similar to: Google giving away Android to capture mobile
   - Risky: Competing with well-funded rivals in a race to bottom

3. **Infrastructure Bet**: Revenue depends on AI Cloud success
   - Businesses choosing Baidu for AI infrastructure
   - Computing power subscriptions as primary revenue
   - Needs enterprise adoption to succeed

**Confidence**: MEDIUM on facts; HIGH on strategic interpretation
